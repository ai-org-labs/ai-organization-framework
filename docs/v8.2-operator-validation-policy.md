# AOF v8.2 Operator Validation Policy

## Purpose

`v8.2.0` makes operator/adopter feedback a governed runtime input.

AOF must not treat tests, dashboards, release tags, or artifact volume as proof
that an operator understood, reproduced, or accepted the work.

## Canonical Artifacts

- `operator-validation-record`: one bounded feedback record from an operator,
  adopter, expert reviewer, self-hosting operator, or simulated operator.
- `operator-validation-audit`: release gate that verifies feedback records are
  linked, reproducible, escalated when unclear, and bounded by not-proven claims.

## Outcome Semantics

- `understood`: the operator can explain what was done and why it matters.
- `reproduced`: the operator can follow the referenced evidence path.
- `accepted`: the operator accepts the bounded claim under the stated boundary.
- `needs_clarification`: the claim is not yet human-clear enough to use.
- `rejected`: the operator does not accept the claim.

## Governance Boundary

Negative, unclear, or not-checked feedback cannot silently pass. It must produce
a governance action: request clarification, request reproduction, block release
claim, or escalate review.

Operator validation does not prove market truth, semantic correctness, broad
adoption, or product value.
